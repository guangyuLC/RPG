In the distant East lies a proud empire, steeped in centuries of tradition and guarded by legends of serpentine dragons that rule the skies. Born of this imperial lineage is Louis, a half-dragon prince said to carry the fiery blood of ancient beasts in his veins.

Throughout your homeland, whispers tell of his inhuman might and the regal dynasty that shaped him—an empire famed for ceremonial pageantry and cunning warfare. Now, with tensions brewing and borders stirred by rumors of war, you have found yourself upon a collision course with this fabled prince.

Fate (or folly) has drawn you both to an ominous forest of forbidden magic, its secrets older than any kingdom. And here, in these haunted woods, your paths will cross, sparking a tale of conflict, destiny, and perhaps a bond neither realm could foresee.

You stand in the heart of the Cursed Woods, feeling ancient magic in the air.

“Who dares trespass here?” A low voice echoes.

A tall figure emerges: dark hair, dark eyes, scales across his arms, and two horns. Louis, the half-dragon prince from an enemy kingdom.

He fixes you with a piercing gaze.

Louis smirks. “State your name and power, intruder.”

Do you reveal yourself as a Witch or an Archer?

“A Witch?” Louis repeats, flicking his gaze over you. “Your magic is nothing compared to dragonfire.”

He challenges you to prove your power.

“An Archer?” Louis narrows his eyes. “I’ve faced many arrows before. None could pierce my dragon scales.”

He demands to see your skill.

You summon a flicker of witchfire between your fingers. Louis’s dark eyes widen slightly.

“Impressive, but still weaker than dragon flame,” he scoffs.

Will you challenge him or attempt diplomacy?

Louis sneers. “Are you too frightened to show your powers?”

Your refusal intrigues him. He steps closer.

Will you stand firm or back away?

You notch an arrow and take aim. Louis stands still, his scales glimmering in the moonlight.

Do you fire or hesitate?

Louis’s horned silhouette looms over you. “You have no will to fight?” he asks in disbelief.

Choose your response:

Your witchfire clashes with Louis’s dragon flame, illuminating the forest in sparks and embers.

He snarls, “You dare challenge me?”

You lower your hands. “I didn’t come here to fight you,” you say quietly.

Louis narrows his eyes. “Then why did you come?”

You stand your ground. Louis circles you warily. “You’re either brave or foolish,” he mutters.

Choose your response:

You step back. Louis steps forward, following your retreat.

“Running away? Where’s your resolve?” he taunts.

You release an arrow that zips past Louis’s shoulder. He doesn’t flinch.

He smirks. “That’s all you’ve got?”

You lower your bow. Louis tilts his head, curiosity gleaming in his eyes.

He approaches slowly.

Louis studies you, scales glinting in the moonlight. “You speak of peace?”

He crosses his arms, waiting for your explanation.

“What did you come here for?” he demands. “You stand in my territory, unafraid.”

You see a flicker of curiosity behind the menace.

Your witchfire intensifies, clashing with Louis’s blazing flame. Sparks swirl, and he’s forced back a step.

He glares, breathing heavily. “Impressive…”

You let your magic subside. Louis inhales, steadying himself. “You yield?” he growls.

But he looks almost relieved. Is he tired of fighting, too?

“I’m here because I seek a cure for the curse on my homeland,” you admit.

Louis’s eyes flicker with something—pity? Understanding?

You remain silent. Louis waits, then scoffs. “Fine, keep your secrets.”

He turns away, tension lingering in the air.

“Brave…” he murmurs. “Or reckless.” He arches a brow. “Which is it?”

Louis huffs a short laugh. “At least you’re honest.”

He seems less hostile now, though still guarded.

Summoning courage, you speak plainly: “Why do you guard these woods so fiercely?”

Louis’s dark eyes flash. “Because it’s all I have left.”

You turn away, intending to leave. Louis calls after you, voice uncertain. “You… don’t want to fight?”

You sense he isn’t used to such refusal.

You rush forward, determined to prove yourself. Louis counters swiftly, a hint of admiration in his gaze.

“Stubborn mortal,” he mutters.

“I have no desire to harm you,” you declare. Louis pauses, heat in his eyes flickering to something like relief.

He lowers his guard.

You reveal the reasons for your presence—seeking knowledge or a cure for your homeland.

Louis listens silently.

Louis stares at you, expression unreadable. Finally, he says, “Why do you remain silent? Speak.”

You describe your desire for peace. Louis listens intently, arms still folded.

You offer a small token—perhaps a charm or relic. Louis accepts it, examining it curiously.

“My homeland is cursed,” you explain. “I’ve come seeking the rumored dragon magic here.”

Louis’s gaze flickers with empathy.

You take a bold step forward, studying him. “What about you? Why guard these woods alone?”

You intensify your witchfire, determined to overcome him. Louis roars in defiance, meeting your power with his flame.

“We need not destroy each other,” you plead. Louis’s chest heaves, but the rage in his eyes diminishes.

“I seek knowledge, not destruction,” you clarify. Louis exhales, releasing tension from his stance.

You remain silent. Louis studies you, then scoffs softly. “Fine. Keep your secrets.”

Yet he doesn’t turn away.

“You’re the only one who can help,” you say. Louis tenses, uncertain.

You insist you don’t need help. Louis bristles. “Foolish mortal,” he mutters, yet he does not leave.

You call after him, voice echoing in the dark woods. He pauses but doesn’t turn.

You let him leave. Silence envelops the clearing—but something in you stirs, urging you to go after him.

“I am braver than you think,” you declare. Louis’s lip quirks in a half-smile.

“Purpose… I had one once,” Louis murmurs. His gaze drifts to the canopy overhead, haunted by memory.

You keep talking, trying to show him you’re not a threat. Slowly, his posture relaxes.

“My kingdom?” Louis’s voice tightens. “It’s gone… destroyed by war.”

You press for details. Louis’s eyes flash with pain. “They took everything,” he growls, voice trembling.

You soften, lowering your voice. “I’m sorry for what you’ve lost.”

Louis exhales shakily.

You explain your reasons: to save your homeland, not to fight. Louis’s anger dims, replaced by curiosity.

You keep walking, but Louis follows, albeit at a distance.

You keep fighting, determined to prove yourself. Louis counters each strike, intense yet never striking to kill.

Night deepens, and you find yourself talking with Louis—sometimes tensely, sometimes in surprising camaraderie.

His dark eyes flicker with curiosity, maybe even respect.

“My father was a great dragon from the East, my mother a human princess,” he says quietly. “I have known neither fully.”

You reveal more about your life, your struggles, and why you ventured here. Louis listens with solemn interest.

Louis’s gaze drifts to the night sky. “I regret not protecting my kingdom,” he murmurs. “This forest is all I have left.”

You sense the loneliness behind his guarded façade.

“My power is both a blessing and a curse,” Louis admits, rubbing the horn on his head. “I am neither fully dragon nor fully human.”

You rest a gentle hand on his scaled arm. “You did what you could,” you say softly. “No one can blame you alone.”

Louis closes his eyes, relief warring with sadness.

You stand quietly beside him, letting your presence speak your empathy. After a moment, he exhales shakily.

“Perhaps… you understand,” he whispers.

You share how you’ve felt torn by your own powers or responsibilities, relating to his half-blood struggle.

Louis’s dark eyes soften.

“My dreams?” he echoes. “I dream of a place where I can exist without being feared or hated.”

He looks at you, a question unspoken.

In the silvered moonlight, you and Louis stand closer than before. Hostility has faded, replaced by something else.

You both realize a connection is forming.

Quiet understanding flows between you. Louis lowers his gaze. “This is new,” he admits. “I never thought I’d trust a human.”

Over the following days, you and Louis share guarded talks, near-confessions, and careful alliances. His regal bearing softens.

You see glimpses of the man behind the dragon scales.

As you walk, Louis points out hidden glades and ancient trees. “I’ve guarded these woods for so long… alone,” he admits.

In a playful spar, you exchange blows—magic or arrows against his draconic strength. Laughter replaces anger.

“You’re not bad,” he says, almost smiling.

Louis’s expression flickers—amusement, then something warmer. “Your spirit is stronger than I realized.”

He meets your gaze, sincerity in his dark eyes. “Perhaps… I admire yours as well.”

Over time, tension transforms into true connection. Louis confides secrets: his father’s draconic legacy, his mother’s lost kingdom.

You realize your hearts are turning toward each other.

You bravely confess: “I never expected to feel this way. Not for someone I was taught to fear.”

Louis’s breath catches. He steps closer.

You hold your tongue, but your eyes say it all. Louis senses it. “There’s something between us,” he murmurs.

He gently touches the scales on his arm, as if afraid to be rejected. “I’m half dragon, half human. I belong nowhere…”

Do you:

“You belong here, with me,” you say, voice trembling with honesty. Louis’s eyes shine.

“I accept all of you,” you whisper. “Your dragon side, your human side—both.”

Louis exhales, relief and hope mixing.

In the hush of the enchanted forest, you and Louis exchange a look that transcends words.

He leans in. “We were enemies,” he murmurs, “yet now…”

“...I cannot imagine letting you go,” he admits softly.

You feel a powerful pull, an unmistakable bond forming.

You tell him your heart has changed too. The forest around you seems to shimmer with quiet approval.

Louis’s arms slip around you, warm with a dragon’s heat. Your heart pounds, realizing how far you’ve come—from enemies to this fragile trust.

His dark eyes reflect the moonlight. “Let me protect you,” he breathes. “Let me stand by your side.”

All your fear dissolves.

You nod, tears of relief burning your eyes. “We protect each other,” you say, voice tight with emotion.

Louis smiles—a real, unguarded smile.

He lowers his head until his forehead rests gently against yours, horn brushing your hair.

A stillness settles, a beat of clarity.

You close your eyes, and in the darkness, you feel Louis’s breath, warm and comforting.

The forest hushes, as if granting you this moment of peace.

Your lips meet in a gentle, tentative kiss that deepens with each heartbeat. Dragon scales brush your skin as Louis draws you closer.

All the tension between your worlds dissolves into this moment, forging a new bond.
